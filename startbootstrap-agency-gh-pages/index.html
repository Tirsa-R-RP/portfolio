"The Helping Hand"

Alert watch for independently living elderly (80 years +)

Project Objective: Improve access to technology, information and services for residents of Malmö over the age of 65, who may otherwise be marginalized due to disability,impairments, age, socioeconomic status, location, etc. This supports the municipality’s goal to improve social sustainability.

Design Process: Explore possible problem areas by conducting street interviews of our target group, find common issues and ideate over them. Sketch possible solutions. Address safety outside the home, concentrating on a wearable so the user doesn't have to take it off and possibly forget. Ideate over the possible wearable devices and decide on the best option. Create a nondescript device that allows the user to feel safe and independent outside the home.

Solution: Design a watch with an alert feature. It serves a dual purpose as a watch and as an alert device. Due to it's stylish design, the user does not feel like their need for this device is on display, avoiding any potential shame factors. It is comfortable and not cumbersome for the user. If the device was accidentally triggered, there is an easy way to cancel the distress call.

  • Date: September 2018
  • Participants: Tirsa RP, Kim S, Martin W, Julija R, Anton F, and Jovan B

Sparks in the Parks

Social activities to facilitate interactions

Project Objective: Improve access to technology, information and services for people who want to increase their social circle but who may otherwise be marginalized due to disability,impairments, age, socioeconomic status, location, etc. This supports the municipality’s goal to improve social sustainability.

Design Process: Explore possible problem areas by conducting interviews in different parks around Malmö. Find common issues for people in the parks and ideate over them. Address the issue of difficulty in meeting others (outside of work and school). Sketch and prototype possible solutions, introduce prototypes and gain feedback from fellow students that are new to Malmö. The concept needs to provide an opportunity where participants feel more comfortable approaching others.

Solution: Create planned, advertised, reoccurring interactive activities in parks throughout Malmö. "Sparks in the Parks" facilitates interactions where participants have a sense of inclusivity and are visibly and socially approachable. Since the events are organized, participants know that if they come across others in the park with a "spark", they are open to interacting. By providing topics of discussions, the artifacts, and organized times, participants should more easily approach others for conversations.

  • Date: October 2018
  • Participants: Tirsa RP, Kim S, Martin W, Julija R, Anton F, and Jovan B

Breakdown Analysis

Analysis of a Mercedes C350e

Project Objective: The goal of this assignment is to analyse an ‘interaction breakdown’ I’ve had with an interactive product using interactivity notions such as; 1) affordances, feedback, feedforward 2) inherent, augmented and functional expressions and 3) ’semantic’ and ‘direct’ meaning.

Analysis: The Knob: false affordance- gear shift? No. The Gear Stick: being in the spot that usually houses the windshield wipers. Then subsequently needing to find the wiper location. The Plate over the knob: hidden affordance-didn’t make susceptibility available. Only learned it was a touch pad after returning the car. The Error prompt: prompt did not give proper feedback. Semantics were unrelated to the alarm. Mercedes assumes that users are familiar with their brand and how they design the interior of their cars. Since it was a rental, I didn’t know how to interact with it properly, despite driving for 12 years in many different types of cars.
Products should aim to be "mistake proof", there were so many times I needed to call for assistance to figure out what was going on. I searched for clues to understand what was going on, but still felt lost. This disengagement and having to think of what to do is an example of a breakdown in the design.

Solution: The Knob + Plate: Introduce feedforward by being illuminated, with lights that move, similar to the “slide to unlock” screen. To allow perceivable affordance. The Gear stick: Provide signifiers; a prompt could appear on the screen if the person has been sitting in the car for a while and still hasn’t interacted with the gear shift. The semantic feedforward prompt could say, “Welcome, gear shift is located behind the steering wheel, have a safe journey.” The Door Open Alarm: The alarm should be accompanied by an icon that shows that a door is open. Or a blinking light at the door to draw your attention to it.

  • Date: September 2018
  • Participants: Tirsa Ramos-Pedersen

PCalc Lite Redesign

Redesign an app for optimal one handed use

Project Objective: Part 1 - analysis: Choose an app on a smartphone (iPhone X) and analyse the interface of it in use. Identify issues with the interface when using one hand to interact, and document these with sketches.
Part 2 - sketching: Re-design the application to solve the issues and optimize it for one-handed use. Part 3 - prototype: Select the best solution for the re-design and produce an interactive digital mock-up of the GUI using Figma.

Design Process: The PCalc Lite app on an iPhone X is difficult to interact with when using one hand. This is due to the size and layout of the screen and buttons. Through sketching, we iterated through many possible redesigns and ultimately built an interactive digital mock-up using Figma.

Solution: We reshaped the buttons into squares so they don’t fill the screen and placed the majority of the keys in Hurff’s “ow”-free zone, which eliminated the need for a “reachability” feature. We decided to place the most common calculator functions on the default screen, providing the user with a button that allows them to access more complex function keys if needed. Once the user has selected the function key in the second screen, they are automatically sent back to the first page to access the numbers. Not having to push a button to go back to the default screen provides the user an uninterrupted experience. There is a scrolling log of the user's calculations under the display panel so s(he) will be able to review their previous calculations.

  • Date: October 2018
  • Participants: Tirsa RP and Richard MS

Product Design

Design children's parties that are educational and entertaining

Project Objective: Develop children's parties that combine MyEsteeme's and CoolMinds values and business goals.

Design Process: Research what was currently available on the market. Research capabilities and design activities for our target group (children aged 4-10). Create artifacts to be used during the parties. Conduct interviews with parents and employees, observe the parties and iterate, redesign activities according to how the children responded to them.

Solution: I developed five individually themed parties based on MyEsteeme characters. Each had age appropriate activities that incorporated the characters' interests.

  • Date: December 2017
  • Participants: Tirsa Ramos-Pedersen
